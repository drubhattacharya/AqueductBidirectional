Evidence checklist — NEMT program claims
37% reduction in missed appointment odds
Shekelle et al. 2022, BMC Public Health — PROSPERO-registered meta-analysis, 12 studies, 3 RCTs. OR 0.63 (95% CI 0.48–0.83). HIGH certainty per GRADE.
25–51% of missed appointments are transportation-attributable
Syed, Gerber & Sharp 2013, Journal of Community Health 38:976–993. Effect concentrates in LMI, disability, and Medicaid populations.
Targeted programs work; universal ones often don't
Chaiyachati RCT, JAMA Internal Medicine 2018 (n=786): universal untargeted Lyft → no reduction (36.5% vs 36.7%). Hennepin Healthcare/Hitch Health targeted model: 27% reduction, 297% ROI.
32% readmission reduction with 30-day post-discharge follow-up
Balasubramanian et al. 2025, JAMA Network Open — 83 studies. RRR 0.68 (95% CI 0.60–0.75). Strongest for AMI (RRR 0.56) and HF (RRR 0.65).
⚠ Direct outcomes attribution is LOW certainty
Berkowitz et al. 2025 (n=86,977, TMLE): −0.09% HbA1c — statistically significant but below 0.5% clinical threshold. NEMT is a necessary enabler within multi-component care models, not standalone.
⚠ No published NEMT + CCM/TCM bundled intervention study
Critical evidence gap. No published research has measured outcomes of NEMT + CCM or NEMT + TCM bundled interventions. The causal chain is supported at each link but not validated end-to-end.
Community need documented in primary federal sources
Verify: Census ACS no-vehicle rate, CDC PLACES uninsured rate, HRSA shortage designation, CHNA published within 3 years. All pulled automatically in Step 02.
Program design is targeted (not universal)
Confirm: program targets patients with prior no-show history OR documented transportation barrier. Universal programs fail (Chaiyachati RCT). Ask for the eligibility criteria in writing.
AKS safe harbor compliance confirmed
42 CFR § 1001.952(bb): written uniform policy; no volume-or-value linkage; no advertising; established patients only; distance limits met. Request signed compliance attestation.
IRS Schedule H reporting path confirmed (Part I Line 7e)
NEMT operating costs should flow to Part I Line 7e (Community Health Improvement Services) — explicitly counts toward community benefit total. Not Part II (which does not count).

Legal & compliance framework
Local transportation safe harbor — 42 CFR § 1001.952(bb)

All 8 conditions must be met simultaneously:

✓
Written, uniformly applied policy — not based on Medicare/Medicaid status
✓
No volume-or-value linkage — transportation not conditioned on referral value
✓
No air, luxury, or ambulance transport — Uber Health and Lyft Healthcare are explicitly permitted
✓
No public advertising — internal patient notification permitted; general advertising is not
✓
Established patients only — must have had at least one prior visit with the entity
✓
Distance limits — ≤25 miles urban / ≤75 miles rural (2020 expansion). No distance limit for discharge-to-home transport (2020 exception).
✓
Medically necessary services only — rides to/from covered healthcare services
✓
Entity bears all costs — cannot shift cost to federal programs
Patient engagement safe harbor (42 CFR § 1001.952(hh)): Permits VBE participants to furnish transportation as in-kind support subject to a $500 annual per-patient cap. No distance limits. Applies within value-based enterprise arrangements.
Medicare Advantage — post-VBID 2026 framework
VBID Model terminated end of 2025. Plans must use the standard MA supplemental benefit framework.
Primarily health-related supplemental benefit (§ 422.102)

Any MA plan may offer transport for medical needs. In 2026: 24% of individual MA plans offer this.

SSBCI — Special Supplemental Benefits for the Chronically Ill (§ 422.102(f))

Transport for non-medical needs for chronically ill enrollees. In 2026: 67% of SNPs offer this. Transportation is NOT on the non-allowable SSBCI list.

Foundation grant compliance
5% payout rule (IRC § 4942)

Private foundations must distribute ≥5% of noncharitable-use assets annually. Both grants and PRIs count. Noncompliance triggers 30% excise tax. For health systems: foundations are required to grant out capital — your NEMT proposal helps them meet a legal obligation.

Expenditure responsibility (IRC § 4945(h))

NOT required for grants to public charities (most nonprofit hospitals = § 509(a)(1)). Required for PRIs to for-profit subsidiaries.

Self-dealing (IRC § 4941)

Foundation cannot transact with "disqualified persons." If a foundation board member also sits on your board, consult counsel before soliciting.
